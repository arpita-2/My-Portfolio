In the far northeastern corner of India lies Arunachal Pradesh, the Wild East of Indian tourism. Within its borders, snow-capped Himalayas encircle pristine golden valleys, and 24 unique tribes are scattered throughout its demanding, but photogenic, terrain. The Shangri La of the northeastern states, it begs to be explored, but is often ignored.   • Nandi Hills

    I visited Nandi Hills on 26th November 2020. Ideal for both a quick getaway and a long refreshing escape, Nandi is an absolute paradise to be in. Being a small hill station in Karnataka, this place can be reached by all modes of commute, i.e. by air, train, and road, but not directly except by road. Basically reach at 6am, to watch the beautiful sunrise. Go at weekends , you will enjoy. I get up and got ready at Early Morning.I started journey at 4:30AM from Avalahalli Bangalore. Nandi Hills, Once I turned in to the Nandi Hills road from the National Highway, the experience was really awsome. On the way to the hill top, I stopped and had tea, you will feel amazing having the cup of tea on cloudy day with fresh air and beautiful morning. After capturing nice view, I went to have breakfast. You will find restaurants with nice taste, I had maggie, vada and of course poori sabji!!! yeah what else you need in your life if you get maggie and tea on top of hill where you surrounded with cloud. I ate some roasted corn also. please wear a jacket or any kind of warmer because it is very cold there Garden are also available there you can roam and BEWARE OF MONKEYS !!! guys. Lots of monkeys are available there so don't do anything with them. You can watch sunset view also. Although the experience of mine was really good.
